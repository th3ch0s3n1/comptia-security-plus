# ⚖️ Section 19: Governance, Policies, & Compliance


> Cornell Notes — CompTIA Security+ (SY0-701) | Maps to Domain 5.0 (Security Program Management and Oversight)


## ❓ Cue / Key Questions & Notes


| Question / Keyword | Answer |
| --- | --- |
| **🟢 Security Frameworks & Standards** | NIST |
| 🔴 What is a security framework? | A structured set of documented guidelines, blueprints, and best practices used to build and manage an organization's cybersecurity posture. |
| 🔴 What is the NIST CSF? | Cybersecurity Framework; a voluntary US framework focused on managing infrastructure risk, built around core functions: Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, and Recover. |
| 🔴 What is ISO/IEC 27001? | An international, auditable standard that defines the structural requirements for establishing and maintaining an Information Security Management System (ISMS). |
| 🔴 What are CIS Benchmarks? | Highly specific, consensus-based secure configuration guides for hardening operating systems, software, cloud environments, and hardware. |
| **🟢 Regulatory Compliance** | GDPR / HIPAA |
| 🔴 What is regulatory compliance? | Adhering to laws, mandates, and industry specifications dictated by government or industry governing bodies. |
| 🔴 What is GDPR? | General Data Protection Regulation; a strict EU data privacy regulation governing how the personal data of EU citizens is collected, stored, and protected globally. |
| 🔴 What is HIPAA? | Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act; a US federal law mandating strict privacy and security protections for protected health information (PHI). |
| 🔴 What is PCI-DSS? | Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard; a strict private industry mandate requiring companies that handle credit cards to maintain secure transaction environments. |
| **🟢 Business & Legal Agreements** | SLA / MOU |
| 🔴 What is an SLA? | Service Level Agreement; a legally binding contract defining the exact performance metrics, uptime guarantees, and penalties expected from a service provider. |
| 🔴 What is an MOU? | Memorandum of Understanding; a formal bilateral agreement between parties outlining a shared intent, mutual goals, and cooperation without an explicit legal contract. |
| 🔴 What is an NDA? | Non-Disclosure Agreement; a legal contract designed to protect intellectual property and proprietary data by prohibiting signees from leaking sensitive info. |
| 🔴 What is a BPA? | Business Partnership Agreement; a legal document detailing the financial stakes, operational responsibilities, and profit-sharing terms between business partners. |
| 🔴 What is an ISA? | Interconnection Security Agreement; a highly technical document specifying the exact security and technical requirements for connecting two distinct networks together. |
| **🟢 Personnel Security Policies** | AUP |
| 🔴 What is an Acceptable Use Policy? | AUP; a foundational document defining the explicit rules and constraints governing how employees may use corporate networks, hardware, and internet resources. |
| 🔴 What is separation of duties? | Splitting a single, high-risk operational task into multiple steps divided among different people to eliminate insider fraud and unauthorized modifications. |
| 🔴 What is job rotation? | Periodically moving employees through different job positions to cross-train staff, reduce burnout, and uncover hidden fraudulent or malicious activities. |
| 🔴 What are mandatory vacations? | Forcing employees to take consecutive days off while another staff member fulfills their role, helping security teams uncover backdoors or internal fraud. |
| 🔴 Onboarding vs. Offboarding | Onboarding sets up accounts and delivers training for new hires; Offboarding revokes all technical access, claims physical badges, and conducts exit interviews. |


## 📝 Summary


**Governance, policies, and compliance** provide the administrative and legal architecture necessary to direct an organization's technical defenses. Corporate environments utilize structured **security frameworks**—such as the core lifecycle functions of the **NIST CSF**, global **ISO/IEC 27001** certification baselines, and highly detailed **CIS Benchmarks**—to guide configuration hardening. These architectures are heavily driven by external legal mandates, requiring continuous compliance assessments against consumer laws like the EU's **GDPR**, healthcare **HIPAA** rules, and finance-driven **PCI-DSS** baselines. Inter-organizational operations secure their relationships through explicit legal instruments, defining service expectations with an **SLA**, shared goals via an **MOU**, asset protections with an **NDA**, partner terms in a **BPA**, and technical perimeter links inside an **ISA**. Finally, internal operational risk is checked through personnel security policies, mandating employee adherence to an **Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)**, and enforcing strict structural checks against insider fraud through **separation of duties**, strategic **job rotation**, **mandatory vacations**, and formalized employee offboarding procedures.
